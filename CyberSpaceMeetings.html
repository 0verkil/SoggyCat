CyberSpace - Meetings

Meetings are the first of four major features of CyberSpace.​
Meetings are your all-in-one improved channel for to make online classes more interesting​
Meetings are extremely easy to set up and work with, here is how...​

Accessibility

Ever had the wrong meeting link, or forgotten to change the meeting settings? With CyberSpace Meetings, these problems will disappear. ​
Meetings utilize a channel that can be joined at specified times, making meeting links unnecessary. (Similar to Discord Voice Channels)​
For middle and high schools, students can chose to be automatically sent to their next period's meetings after the previous one ended​
Meetings also have extended features that keeps students participating and engaged

Hand Raising​

Meetings are easily managed by teachers. Other than having the essential mute/unmute start/stop video, we also have a better hand raising system​
Teachers can enable hand raising when they want to, and students raise their hands just by clicking the raise hand button​
Teachers call on people by clicking the hand icon on the student profile and once called, all held hands go down so they don’t stay up at other times​
Rather than hands popping up next to participants in order from the fastest hand raise, teachers can see who got called on the least, and generate more participation from students​
This participation number can also been seen on participants not raising their hands, so if those raising their hands have been called on to many times, teachers can choose a random person who has no participated​

Automatic Attendance​

The teacher is provided a list of students who are in the class​.
As students join, they will be marked as present​.
People who join late will be marked as tardy with a note for when they joined​.
People who don’t attend the meeting for more than a set amount of time marked absent​.
The teacher can also edit attendance manually.
Students receive a notification if they were tardy or absent, and can respond to the notification, and explain why they were late or absent.​

Staying on Task​

It is up to the teachers whether or not students are required to have cameras on. However, even with their cameras on, there is a significant possibility the student is distracted by other applications on their device.​
CyberSpace has built in AFK Detection, notifying a teacher if a student is particularly disengaged, by detecting if the teacher-selected windows are not in focus. ​
If they are for a long period of time, it will notify the teacher, and the teacher can remind the student to stay on task or make them prove they are active
